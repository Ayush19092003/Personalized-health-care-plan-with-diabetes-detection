Pre-diabetic

Step Procedure Details
Diet Management Adopt a Low-Carb, High-Fiber Diet Reduce sugar and refined carbs. Eat whole grains, vegetables, lean proteins, and fiber-rich foods.
Portion Control Avoid Overeating Use smaller plates, follow the Plate Method, and eat slowly.
Regular Exercise Engage in Physical Activity Aim for 150 minutes of moderate exercise per week, including strength training.
Weight Management Maintain a Healthy Weight Losing 5-10% of body weight can significantly reduce diabetes risk.
Hydration Drink Plenty of Water Avoid sugary drinks; aim for at least 8 glasses of water per day.
Sleep & Stress Management Prioritize Sleep & Reduce Stress Get 7-9 hours of sleep and manage stress through relaxation techniques.
Blood Sugar Monitoring Track Blood Glucose Levels Check fasting blood glucose regularly and maintain a glucose journal.
Medical Consultation Work with Healthcare Professionals Have regular checkups and consider HbA1c testing every 3-6 months.
Lifestyle Changes Avoid Smoking & Alcohol Quit smoking and limit alcohol intake for better insulin function.

Type 1

Category Plan of Action
Medical Evaluation & Monitoring Doctor Consultation: Regular check-ups every 3–6 months.
Glucose Monitoring: Check fasting and post-meal blood sugar daily or at least 3 times a week.
HbA1c Test: Every 3 months (goal: <7%).
Lipid Profile & Kidney Tests: Annually to check for complications.
Diet & Nutrition Low Glycemic Index (GI) Foods: Whole grains, vegetables, lean proteins, and healthy fats.
Portion Control: Avoid large carb-heavy meals, eat small frequent meals.
Reduce Sugar & Processed Foods: No sodas, sweets, white bread, or excessive fried food.
Hydration: Drink 2–3 liters of water daily.
Physical Activity Exercise for 30–45 minutes daily (brisk walking, cycling, swimming, yoga).
Strength Training 2–3 times a week to improve insulin sensitivity.
Avoid Sedentary Lifestyle: Take breaks from long sitting periods.
Medications (if needed) Metformin (first-line medication to reduce insulin resistance).
DPP-4 inhibitors (e.g., Sitagliptin) if additional control is needed.
No need for insulin at this stage unless directed by a doctor.
Weight Management Maintain a healthy BMI (18.5–24.9).
Target weight loss of 5–10% if overweight.
Stress Management & Sleep Reduce Stress: Meditation, deep breathing, or therapy if needed.
Sleep 7–8 hours per night to regulate blood sugar.
Complication Prevention Regular Eye Check-ups (to prevent diabetic retinopathy).
Foot Care (check for wounds or infections).
Blood Pressure & Cholesterol Monitoring to prevent heart disease.
Emergency Plan If blood sugar rises above 200 mg/dL consistently, consult a doctor immediately.
Watch for symptoms like excessive thirst, frequent urination, fatigue, or blurred vision.

Type 2

Category Plan of Action
Medical Evaluation & Monitoring Immediate doctor consultation for treatment adjustment.
Frequent glucose monitoring (before meals and bedtime).
HbA1c test every 3 months (goal: <7%).
Kidney function, lipid profile, and liver tests every 6 months.
Diet & Nutrition Strict low-carb, high-fiber diet with lean proteins and healthy fats.
Eliminate refined sugars, white bread, and processed foods.
Meal portion control with balanced macronutrient intake.
Increase water intake (2.5–3 liters per day) to prevent dehydration.
Physical Activity Low-impact aerobic exercises like walking, swimming, or cycling.
Strength training to improve insulin sensitivity.
Avoid excessive strain; consult a doctor before intense exercise.
Medications Insulin therapy may be required for better glucose control.
Adjust oral medications such as Metformin, Sulfonylureas, or SGLT2 inhibitors.
Doctor may prescribe GLP-1 receptor agonists (e.g., Liraglutide) for weight and glucose control.
Weight Management Maintain a BMI within 18.5–24.9 range.
Target weight loss of 5–15% for overweight individuals.
Stress Management & Sleep Reduce stress through meditation, yoga, or therapy.
Ensure 7–9 hours of quality sleep per night.
Complication Prevention Regular eye exams to prevent diabetic retinopathy.
Foot care to check for infections or ulcers.
Blood pressure and cholesterol management to prevent cardiovascular disease.
Emergency Plan If glucose exceeds 300 mg/dL, seek emergency medical attention.
Watch for symptoms of diabetic ketoacidosis (DKA) or hyperosmolar hyperglycemic state (HHS).
Hydrate and follow doctor’s emergency insulin correction plan.
